Challenge

Why generic robotics data breaks in hospitality

Hospitality environments are dense with shifting intent, social cues, and dynamic human movement. Generic datasets underrepresent these behaviors, causing brittle model performance in guest-facing settings.

Social context is missing

Intent-rich interactions such as greetings, handoffs, and turn-taking are usually weakly represented in broad datasets.

Environments are dynamic

Cafes and hotels add crowding, occlusions, and noise patterns that differ from lab-controlled data.

Task + interaction coupling

Service tasks are embedded in social exchanges; models need both dimensions jointly captured.
